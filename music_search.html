Search Tips:
 

Type keywords or truncated words. Use the limiters or pull-down field searching menus to narrow search results.

 

Word Order / Phrase Searching

 

Keywords are searched separately. To search words as a phrase, enclose them in quotation marks.

Examples:

"rite of spring"
"John Philip Sousa"

 

Truncation

 

Use an asterisk to look for 1-5 characters after a word stem. Use a double asterisk to search for an unlimited number of characters.

A question mark (?) can be used to look for an unknown character within a word.

Examples:

mus* - finds music, musical, musician, muse, museum, etc.
mus** - finds musicians, musicality, etc.
wom?n - finds women and woman

 

Connectors / Boolean Searching

 

Use AND to separate keywords when using multiple operators.

Use OR to broaden your search by looking for any of several words.  These words must be nested in parentheses.

Use AND NOT to eliminate specific words from your search.

Examples:
 
oboe AND (flute OR clarinet) AND NOT violin

 

Proximity Searching

 

Use NEAR to search for words within 10 terms of each other, in any order.

Use WITHIN # to search for words occurring within the entered number of words of each other, in any order.

Use BEFORE and AFTER to search for words in a particular order

Examples:

mass NEAR war
missa WITHIN 3 bella
rite BEFORE spring
